## Introduction

In today’s competitive business environment, effective supply chain management is essential for driving operational efficiency, controlling costs, and maximizing profitability. This project involved the comprehensive analysis of a supply chain dataset for a consumer goods company operating in the personal care sector across major Indian metropolitan markets.

Using Microsoft Excel as the primary analytical platform, the raw operational data was cleaned, transformed, and structured to enable in-depth performance evaluation. The analysis focused on key business dimensions including product categories, supplier performance, geographic locations, logistics modes, quality metrics, and financial outcomes.

This Excel-driven initiative demonstrates the ability to convert complex operational data into clear, actionable business intelligence, supporting strategic decision-making in supply chain and operations management.

## Overview of Work

This project involved the end-to-end processing and analysis of a transformed supply chain dataset containing **100 SKUs** across three product categories (Skincare, Haircare, and Cosmetics), five suppliers, and five major Indian cities (Mumbai, Kolkata, Chennai, Bangalore, and Delhi).

### Dataset Scope
- **Records analyzed**: 100 unique SKUs
- **Product Categories**: Skincare, Haircare, Cosmetics
- **Geographic Coverage**: 5 major Indian metropolitan locations
- **Key Dimensions Analyzed**: Revenue, Quantity Sold, Stock Levels, Profit Margins, Defect Rates, Supplier Performance, Logistics Costs, and Transportation Modes

## Step-by-Step Procedure

The project followed a structured Excel-based analytical workflow:

### 1. Data Acquisition and Initial Review
- Imported the supply chain dataset into Microsoft Excel.
- Conducted initial data review to understand structure, column headers, data types, and overall completeness.

### 2. Data Cleaning
- Applied filters and sorting to identify inconsistencies and anomalies.
- Used Find & Replace, Text to Columns, and Remove Duplicates functions to standardize data.
- Applied Data Validation rules to ensure consistency in categorical fields (e.g., Product Type, Location, Supplier, Transport Mode).
- Verified numeric fields for accuracy and logical consistency.

### 3. Data Transformation
- Created calculated columns using Excel formulas to derive key metrics such as Profit Margin and Total Cost.
- Structured the data into Excel Tables to enable dynamic referencing and easier analysis.
- Prepared the dataset for multi-dimensional summarization.

### 4. Pivot Table Development and Analysis
- Created multiple PivotTables to aggregate and analyze data across key business dimensions:
  - Revenue and Quantity Sold by Product Category
  - Performance by Supplier and Geographic Location
  - Revenue breakdown by Transportation Mode
  - Stock levels and Order Quantities by City
- Utilized grouping, calculated fields, and value field settings within PivotTables to generate meaningful summaries.
- Developed the summary Pivot Table sheet for quick reference and reporting.

### 5. KPI Calculation and Performance Evaluation
- Calculated core KPIs including Total Revenue, Average Profit Margin, Average Defect Rate, and other operational metrics.
- Segmented performance by product category, supplier, location, and transport mode.
- Applied conditional formatting to highlight high-performing and underperforming areas.

### 6. Visualization and Reporting
- Created charts (bar charts, pie charts) directly from PivotTables to visualize key trends and comparisons.
- Compiled summary tables and insights into a clear, professional format suitable for stakeholder review.
- Documented methodology, assumptions, and key findings for reproducibility.

### 7. Documentation and Final Deliverables
- Prepared the final transformed Excel workbook containing the cleaned data and comprehensive Pivot Table summaries.
- Created this professional project documentation to communicate the approach, findings, and recommendations.

### Key Findings
- Skincare emerged as the primary revenue driver and should be prioritized for inventory optimization and demand planning.
- Rail transportation generated the highest revenue share, indicating it offers an effective balance of cost and reliability for many routes.
- The portfolio achieved a strong average profit margin of 86.07%, while the 2.28% average defect rate presents an opportunity for targeted supplier quality improvements.
- Performance variations across suppliers and locations suggest potential for deeper analysis and optimization of sourcing and distribution strategies.

### Recommendations
1. Prioritize inventory and demand forecasting efforts for high-revenue skincare SKUs.
2. Review supplier performance, particularly those contributing to higher total costs, to identify opportunities for negotiation or alternative sourcing.
3. Evaluate transportation mode efficiency and consider route consolidation where appropriate.
4. Implement enhanced quality monitoring processes with suppliers showing higher defect rates to further reduce quality-related costs.
5. Develop recurring Excel-based dashboards for ongoing monitoring of key supply chain KPIs.
